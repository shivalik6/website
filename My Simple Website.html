About SOHAL

SOHAL village is located in Akhnoor tehsil of Jammu district in Jammu & Kashmir, India. It is situated 10km away from sub-district headquarter Akhnoor and 40km away from district headquarter Jammu. According to the locals, the village got this name because 16 (sola) castes lived together in the village. But according to the historical records found, during the rule of Raja Aalam Singh, Maharaja Ranjit Singh annexed Akhnoor and gave 16 villages, i.e., Sohal, as jagir to him. The total geographical area of the village is 915 hectares. Sohal has a total population of 3,880 people, out of which the male population is 1,955 while the female population is 1,925. The literacy rate of Sohal village is 65.64%, out of which 72.63% males and 58.55% females are literate. There are about 900 houses in Sohal village. The Pincode of Sohal village locality is 181201. Akhnoor is the nearest town to Sohal for all major economic activities, which is approximately 10km away. Public bus (mini-bus) service from Akhnoor to Sohal is available.

Places to visit

Our village boasts several serene parks, picturesque ponds, and beautiful temples that attract visitors and locals alike. Among these, the most famous is the baba sehaj nath ji mandir.

baba sehaj nath ji mandir: This revered temple, dedicated to puran bhagat also known as baba sehaj nath ji, Baba Sahaj Nath Ji, is the supreme head of the Jandiyals, a Hindu caste. The Jandiyals gather twice a year on Guru Purnima and worship sehaj nath ji.

Village Park: Our village park is a popular spot for relaxation and recreation, offering lush green spaces and a peaceful environment.Many people come to the park early in the morning walk.In the evening children play cricket in the park.

Village Pond: The village pond is a serene spot where locals gather for fishing, picnics, and enjoying the natural beauty of our surroundings.
